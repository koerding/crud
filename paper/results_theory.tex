\subsection{Bridge lemma: the crud scale and its decay}

We formalize the link from the eigenvalue spectrum to the residual correlation distribution. Theorem~\ref{thm:crud-scale} (SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas}) shows that under a Haar-like delocalization condition on the eigenvectors of the covariance, the SD of the residual correlation $\rho^{(K)}_{ij}$ across uniformly random pairs is well approximated by a ratio of eigenvalue tail sums:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigma-K}
\sigma_K \;:=\; \frac{\sqrt{\sum_{k>K}\lambda_k^2}}{\sum_{k>K}\lambda_k}.
\end{equation}
The numerator measures how unevenly the remaining variance is distributed across components; the denominator sets the scale. Eigenvector delocalization is met only approximately in our datasets ($3.8$--$11.2\times$ the Haar ideal; SI Appendix~\ref{app:assumption-audit}, Test~2), but the resulting bias is conservative for the crud-scale claim (localization tends to make~\eqref{eq:sigma-K} overestimate the typical residual correlation; SI Appendix~\ref{app:approx-robust}).

If the eigenvalues follow a power law $\lambda_k \approx c k^{-\alpha}$, then $\sigma_K$ decays slowly: in the especially relevant $\alpha \approx 1$ case, $\sigma_K \approx 1/(\sqrt{K}\log(p/K))$ (Corollary~\ref{cor:powerlaw}, SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas}). Removing $K=10$ components can leave a substantial residual crud scale when $p$ is large, formalizing why low-rank adjustment shrinks the correlation distribution only modestly.

\subsection{Decision-theoretic limit}

A complementary question: given that background correlations are drawn from a distribution of width $\sigma_K$, can any rule reliably classify a pair as causally linked versus correlated by background dependence? Theorem~\ref{thm:assoc-only} (SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas}) formalizes this as a signal-detection problem. Under a stylized Gaussian mixture in which a direct causal edge induces a mean shift $\mu$ in the adjusted association statistic $T_{ij}$, the minimum achievable misclassification error for any rule that uses only $T_{ij}$ is $\Phi(-|\mu|/(2\sigma_K))$. If $|\mu|$ is at most a constant multiple of $\sigma_K$, this error is bounded below by a constant independent of sample size. The limitation persists as $n\to\infty$ within our model: under fixed $K$, $\sigma_K$ is a population quantity that more samples cannot reduce. Whether this model-level quantity matches the conceptually natural notion of ``background dependence'' in a given domain depends on whether the chosen adjustment captures that domain's confounding structure (SI Appendix~\ref{si:test-details}). Sparse causal structure ($\pi \ll 1$) further compounds the limitation by collapsing positive predictive value (SI Appendix~\ref{si:base-rate}). Heavy-tailed residuals, additional variance structure, and selected target pairs are discussed in SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas} and SI Appendix~\ref{si:test-details}.
