\section{Discussion}

When reported adjusted associations are comparable to the crud scale, causal interpretation is fragile without additional design leverage, even if classical $p$-values are tiny. The crud-aware test provides a minimal magnitude standard for deciding when a small association is worth interpreting. As positive controls, smoking and lung cancer (RR$\approx 7$; converted to a Pearson correlation via Eq.~\eqref{eq:rr-to-r} of SI Appendix~\ref{app:scale-bridge} at representative population prevalences $p_X{=}0.20$, $p_Y{=}0.06$, this gives $r\approx 0.28$, or $\approx 3\sigma_K$ against NHANES at $K=10$) and within-facet HEXACO personality items (best pair $>$99.99th percentile) clear the threshold; SI Appendix~\ref{si:smoking-detail} gives the structural-ceiling caveat for binary correlations and the case-control-vs-cohort distinction. Crucially, $3\sigma_K$ alone is not what makes smoking--cancer compelling: decades of converging dose--response, cessation, mechanism, and animal evidence carry that case. The crud-aware test is a magnitude diagnostic, not a sole filter for causal credibility.

\paragraph{Scope and leverage.} Prior knowledge or design-based leverage effectively isolates variables from background dependence. Randomized treatments, discontinuities, policy changes, and valid instruments provide leverage that is orthogonal to generic correlation structure. Informed selection of which pairs to examine---from prior biology, mechanism, or pathway databases---narrows the empirical null; our impossibility result applies to the association statistic itself, not to the prior that selected the pair. Our results do not argue against such approaches; they target association-only workflows that interpret small adjusted pairwise correlations as causal evidence in the absence of additional leverage. Within the stylized Gaussian-mixture model of Theorem~\ref{thm:assoc-only} (SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas}), rules that compress each pair to a one-dimensional adjusted-association statistic incur irreducible error when the causal signal is comparable to the crud scale; systems that exploit multivariate structure, nonlinear dependence, mechanistic priors, or interventions are outside the scope of this bound. The homogeneity of broad spectra and slow $\sigma_K$ shrinkage across our nine datasets is consistent with the obstacle generalizing beyond a single domain or modality; confirming truly cross-domain status requires a broader survey than nine cases can provide.

\paragraph{Limitations.} All results concern linear dependence (correlations, linear PC adjustment, covariance spectra). In domains with substantial nonlinear dependence---gene regulatory networks, neural population codes, epidemiological dose--response---$\sigma_K$ is a lower bound on the true background dependence; whether nonlinear adjustment would shrink the nonlinear background faster than linear adjustment shrinks the linear background is not tested here. The quantitative $\sigma_K$ varies across domains and preprocessing; we use PC regression as a deliberately generic adjustment, not a claim of causal identification. In high-dimensional regimes ($p$ not small relative to $n$), estimating dominant directions adds uncertainty to $\sigma_K$ that the test does not propagate (SI Appendix~\ref{app:alt-adjust}). The decision-theoretic limit is per-pair, equal-variance, Gaussian, and fixed-$K$. SI Appendix~\ref{si:practical-stakes} discusses asymmetric costs of skepticism in concrete settings (e.g., NIH R01 marginal-grant analysis).
