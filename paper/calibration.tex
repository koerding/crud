\subsection{Crud-aware testing}

The empirical results above imply a minimum reporting standard for small-effect observational claims: compare any adjusted association to the same-pipeline empirical null---the distribution of adjusted associations over uniformly sampled feature pairs from the same dataset, computed under identical preprocessing and adjustment. The resulting crud-aware $p$-value is the fraction of random pairs whose absolute adjusted association exceeds the target's. Associations below a chosen high percentile of this null (conventionally the 95th) should be reported as not distinctive against the domain background; the cutoff is conventional, not principled. The test is a magnitude diagnostic: failing to clear it means the magnitude alone cannot be cleanly distinguished from typical residual dependence, not that the relation is causally unimportant (mediation, suppression, and confounder-driven attenuation can produce small adjusted correlations from causally important relations); clearing it is necessary, not sufficient, for a causal interpretation. SI Section~\ref{si:test-details} gives details, the handling of selected target pairs (which violate exchangeability), stratified comparisons, and the relation to multiple-testing corrections.

\subsection{A parametric crud-aware \texorpdfstring{$z$}{z}-test}

Many studies measure only a few variables on a small sample. Such a study can be understood as a small submatrix of the much larger data matrix one could have collected; the crud factor operates at the domain level. Under a Gaussian approximation (Theorem~\ref{thm:parametric-crud}, SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas}), the total variance of $r$ for a random pair from the domain is the sum of crud and sampling contributions, $\Var(r) \approx \sigma_K^2 + 1/(n-1)$, where $\sigma_K$ is the population residual-correlation SD defined in Eq.~\ref{eq:sigma-K}. This yields the test statistic
\begin{equation}\label{eq:z-crud-main}
z_{\mathrm{crud}} = \frac{|r|}{\sqrt{\sigma_K^2 + 1/(n-1)}},
\end{equation}
with two-sided $p$-value $p_{\mathrm{crud}} = 2\,\Phi(-z_{\mathrm{crud}})$. The classical correlation test is the special case $\sigma_K=0$. As $n$ grows, $1/(n-1)$ shrinks but $\sigma_K^2$ does not, so for the purpose of distinguishing small associations from background dependence there are diminishing returns past $n \approx 1/\sigma_K^2$ (SI Appendix~\ref{si:diminishing-returns}). The Gaussian formula is broadly calibrated at the conventional 5\% threshold and loses calibration for $p_{\mathrm{crud}}<0.01$ in heavy-tailed domains; for stronger claims use the empirical null (SI Appendix~\ref{app:bridge-lemmas}, Table~\ref{tab:heavy-tail-cal}; SI Appendix~\ref{si:parametric-calibration}). A step-by-step reporting workflow, an online calculator, and guidance on choosing $\sigma_K$ for an applied study are in SI Appendix~\ref{si:how-to-use}.

\subsection{Worked example: folate--mortality in T2D}
\label{sec:worked-examples}

\citet{liu2022} report a nominally significant association between folate and mortality in adults with type-2 diabetes ($r \approx 0.02$, $p_{\mathrm{classical}} \approx 0.04$, $n \approx 8{,}000$). With NHANES $\sigma_K=0.093$ at $K=10$, the crud-aware $z$-test gives $z_{\mathrm{crud}}=0.24$, $p_{\mathrm{crud}}=0.81$: the effect's magnitude is typical of randomly chosen NHANES pairs after the same adjustment. By contrast, within-facet HEXACO item pairs (designed to load on the same trait) clear the threshold easily ($p_{\mathrm{crud}}=0.03$ for the median pair). SI Appendix~\ref{si:worked-examples-extended} gives both examples in full and reports two further cases (NHANES biomarkers, GTEx cis-eQTL effects).
